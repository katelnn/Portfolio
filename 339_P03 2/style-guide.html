About Myself

Hello! my name is Kate Luong and I'm a passionate UI and UX designer based in Vancouver, Canada. With a blend of creativity, technical skills and the passion for design, my skills lies in the art of creative ideation and crafting tailored solutions that resonates with a wide range of audience.

Currently, I'm studying at Simon Fraser University in the Interactive Arts and Technology program. Through my education journey, I've had the priviledge of conceptualizing and executing a myriad of projects ranging from topics like Psychology, Computer Science, 3D modeling and Human-Computer Interactions. My academic pursuits have provided me with invaluable insights and hands-on experience, and each project has been a voyage of discovery which I can bring into my designs.

Projects

Project 1: A Microsite about The Vancouver International Film Festival

This project aims to address a pressing issue faced by our chosen client, the Vancouver International Film Festival (VIFF), by developing a microsite tailored to tackle the identified challenge. Serving as both the UI designer and prototyper, my role involves crafting the digital interface and refining the user experience.

Through extensive research, we've observed the profound transformation undergone by the film exhibition industry in the digital age. This evolution has redefined the significance of arthouses and festivals like VIFF, necessitating a strategic reevaluation of their offerings to maintain relevance. To thrive in this dynamic landscape, VIFF must modernize its digital infrastructure and human resources practices to ensure competitiveness and long-term sustainability.

Our research question, "How can we revitalize in-person experiences for younger audiences amidst the prevalence of digital interactions?", underscores our commitment to fostering genuine connections in an increasingly virtual world. In response, we've conceived the "Digital Passport Program," which introduces tangible artifacts for attendees to take home, fostering lasting connections beyond the digital realm. Drawing inspiration from Jakob's law, which advocates for leveraging familiar design patterns and cognitive models, we've adopted a user-centric approach to ensure the experience is more interactive for the audience.

Project 1: Ideation

This project centers around crafting a post-event microsite dedicated to showcasing the vibrant lineup of artists featured in the 5X Festival, a celebration of South Asian music and culture. Our client, 5X FEST, is deeply committed to uplifting South Asian youth culture, yet we've identified a gap in their online presence. Their current website lacks comprehensive information about the featured artists and their representation of South Asian culture, resulting in a predominantly South Asian audience.

Our overarching objective is to broaden the festival's reach and foster inclusivity by posing the question: "How can we effectively promote and expand the visibility of South Asian culture to a wider and more diverse audience?"

By designing a microsite spotlighting individual artists, we not only uphold 5X FEST's mission of championing creativity but also work towards amplifying opportunities for South Asian individuals and people of color in the arts and culture sector. In our design process, we conscientiously integrate elements that resonate with South Asian themes, colors, and graphics, while infusing modern aesthetics through unconventional layout choices. Our approach embraces neo-brutalism, a design philosophy that challenges traditional norms by juxtaposing bold colors and unconventional placement of elements. This stylistic fusion not only pays homage to South Asian design traditions but also resonates with contemporary youth culture, fostering a sense of progressivism and inclusivity.
